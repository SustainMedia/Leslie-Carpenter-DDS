What Causes Teeth to Stain?

Your teeth can become yellow or stained for many reasons:

  • Drinks like coffee, tea, and red wine
  • Foods with strong colors like berries and tomato sauce
  • Smoking or chewing tobacco
  • Some medicines
  • Getting older
  • Injury to a tooth

Even with good brushing, these stains can build up over time. Professional whitening removes these stains in ways that regular brushing cannot.

The Teeth Whitening Process at Our Office

When you come in for teeth whitening, we first check your teeth to make sure they're healthy. We'll protect your gums with a special covering, then apply the whitening gel to your teeth. A special light may be used to activate the gel and speed up the whitening process. The whole treatment usually takes about an hour.

If you choose a take-home kit, we'll make custom trays that fit your teeth perfectly. You'll get professional-strength whitening gel and clear instructions on how to use it safely at home.

Is Teeth Whitening Right for You?

Teeth whitening works best for people with healthy teeth and gums. If you have cavities or gum problems, we'll need to fix those first. Some types of discoloration don't respond well to whitening, especially if they're caused by medicines or injury. During your visit, we'll honestly tell you what results you can expect.

Maintaining Your Bright Smile

After whitening your teeth, you'll want to keep them looking bright. We'll give you tips on how to prevent new stains, such as:

  • Using a straw when drinking coffee, tea, or soda
  • Rinsing with water after eating or drinking staining foods
  • Brushing or rinsing soon after meals
  • Getting regular dental cleanings
