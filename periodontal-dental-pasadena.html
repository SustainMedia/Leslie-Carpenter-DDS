What is Periodontal Disease?

Periodontal disease is a fancy name for gum disease. It happens when the gums around your teeth get infected. This starts when sticky stuff called plaque builds up on your teeth. If you don't clean it off, it can make your gums red, puffy, and sore. Over time, it can damage the support for your teeth and even lead to tooth loss. But don't worry - Dr. Leslie Carpenter can help you fight gum disease and keep your smile healthy.

Signs You Might Need Periodontal Care:

  • Gums that bleed when you brush or floss
  • Red, swollen, or tender gums
  • Gums that have pulled away from your teeth
  • Bad breath that won't go away
  • Loose teeth or teeth that are moving apart
  • Changes in how your teeth fit together when you bite

If you notice any of these signs, it's important to come see us soon. The earlier we treat gum problems, the easier they are to fix.

Understanding Periodontal Treatment:

When you come for periodontal care, we first look carefully at your gums. We might take special pictures of your teeth and measure the pockets around each tooth. This helps us understand how serious your gum disease is.

For mild gum disease, a regular cleaning and better home care might be enough. For more serious problems, we might need to do a deep cleaning. This removes bacteria and buildup from below your gumline where your toothbrush can't reach.

After treatment, we'll set up a plan to keep your gums healthy. This might mean coming back more often for cleanings. Don't worry - each visit gets you closer to healthy gums and a stronger smile.

Why Healthy Gums Matter:

Your gums are the foundation for your teeth. Without healthy gums, even the strongest teeth can become loose and fall out. But the importance of gum health goes beyond your mouth. Research shows that gum disease is linked to other health problems like heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. Taking care of your gums helps your whole body stay healthier.
