Signs You May Need Wisdom Teeth Removal:

  • Pain or swelling at the back of your mouth
  • Pressure on nearby teeth
  • Red, swollen, or bleeding gums around back teeth
  • Jaw pain or difficulty opening your mouth
  • Ongoing bad breath or strange taste
  • Crowding or shifting of other teeth
  • Frequent headaches originating near the jaw

Even if you don't have symptoms, your dentist might recommend removal if your X-rays show potential future problems.

Why Remove Wisdom Teeth?

Wisdom teeth often cause problems because most people's mouths don't have enough room for them. When wisdom teeth try to come in without enough space, they can:

  • Grow sideways into nearby teeth
  • Get stuck partially under the gums (impacted)
  • Create pockets where bacteria grow
  • Push other teeth out of alignment
  • Form cysts that damage bone and roots

Removing problematic wisdom teeth early prevents these issues and is usually easier than waiting until problems develop.

When Is the Best Time for Wisdom Teeth Removal?

Most dentists recommend removing wisdom teeth between ages 16-25, when the roots aren't fully formed and removal is easier. However, wisdom teeth can be removed at any age if they're causing problems.

During your regular dental checkups, we monitor your wisdom teeth development and can recommend the best timing for your specific situation.

Recovery After Wisdom Teeth Removal

Most people return to normal activities within a few days after wisdom teeth removal. Here's what to expect:

  • The first 24-48 hours: Some swelling and discomfort
  • Days 3-7: Gradual improvement
  • Week 2: Most patients feel back to normal

We'll provide specific instructions for eating, drinking, and cleaning your mouth during recovery. Following these instructions carefully helps prevent complications like dry socket.
