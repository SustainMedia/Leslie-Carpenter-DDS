What is Periodontal (Gum) Disease?

Gum disease starts when sticky plaque builds up along your gumline. This causes:

  • Red, swollen, or bleeding gums
  • Bad breath that won't go away
  • Gums pulling away from teeth
  • Loose teeth or changes in how teeth fit together

Many Pasadena patients don't notice early signs. That's why regular check-ups with Dr. Carpenter are so important. Early gum disease (gingivitis) can be reversed, but advanced disease (periodontitis) requires ongoing care. Left untreated, gum disease can lead to tooth loss and affect your overall health.

Why Periodontal Care Matters for Pasadena Residents

  • Diabetes complications
  • Respiratory issues
  • Pregnancy complications

Your Periodontal Maintenance Plan

After treating gum disease, keeping it under control is crucial. Your personalized maintenance plan typically includes:

  • More frequent cleaning visits (every 3-4 months)
  • Special home care instructions and tools
  • Regular checks of your gum pocket depths
  • Ongoing monitoring of your overall oral health

With Dr. Carpenter's guidance and your good home care, we can help maintain your periodontal health and protect your natural teeth for years to come.
